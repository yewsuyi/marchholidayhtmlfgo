How to farm with Scathach-Skadi the chad way

What is Skadi 3 turn farming?

Skadi 3 turn farming is the art of clearing mob waves (typically ember farming) using 1 turn for each battle by utilising a Skadi of your own and a Friend Support Skadi. This is much less mentally taxing than conventional forms of farming as the game plays by itself 80% of the time.

Casting Skadi's S1 Quick Steroid twice on the Qdps allows their NPs to overkill all enemies and subsequently obtain an NP refund of above 50%. Skadi's S3 50% NP battery, "The All-Father's Wisdom", is then used on turn 2 and turn 3 to refill a Quick DPS' NP gauge fully, allowing them to NP 3 times consecutively.

A buff-stacked Valkyrie (leftmost) being used to farm QP

Why learn this?

3 turn farming with 2 Skadis is a luxury many cannot afford, and even one some whales cannot indulge in. Only learning how to farm this way is elitist and makes you a brainless farmer.

3 turn farming also costs a lot on your end. Using 2 Skadis means having to grind your Skadi's quick steroid and NP battery to a decent level, which costs a lot of materials, time, and most importantly QP. Just one level lower could mean the difference between a 99% NP charge roundup or 2 additional turns to clean the trash. In most cases, limit-broken or high rarity CEs (such as Kaleidoscope) are also required.

Furthermore, the essence of a dual Skadi setup is using a dps NP 3 times, which wastes a lot of time because NP animations are unskippable and REALLY SLOW. Using 1 Skadi actually reduces this to doing dps NPs 1.5 times.

Finally, it absolutely cripples your ability to level up good mystic codes. Most Quick dpses do not have a substantial self NP battery to make use of a turn 1 Kaleidoscope, which means that Masters have to supplement with mana transfer. This forces farming to only be done with MCs that have a battery, preventing the natural levelling up of actually useful MCs like the black one you unlock in the Russian Lostbelt, particularly because an ideal 3 turn setup gives you 4 CE slots to boost MC xp gained at the end of a battle.

So how does one become a chad Skadi farmer?

This farming technique replaces your Skadi with Arash, a 1-star Archer. Although his base atk may be laughably low, his Buster AOE NP, at level 5, does more damage than any other servant in the game (I think), allowing him to annihilate max level Lancer-class embers against class advantage (with MC buffs). His low rarity means that you have probably already gotten more than 10 dupes of him, allowing even low level Masters to get him to NP5. The best part about Stella, his NP, is that Arash kills himself immediately after using it, allowing you to bring another proper DPS into the field after clearing the first wave.

Turn 2 follows the conventional 2 Skadi technique of steroiding a Qdps and having him NP. If done correctly, you will begin the final turn with more than 50 critical stars, which means that all your command cards will result in a critical attack. Your 5 cards will each have a 67% percent chance of coming from a dps, which means you will mop the floor 90% of the time that turn, and then 99% of the time on turn 4.

Lineup:

  • Quick DPS (refer to table below), any CE with NP of at least 50%
  • Arash, NP5, S3lvl5, imaginary element (lb)
  • Support Skadi, S1lvl10, S3lvl10
  • Any DPS (with class advantage)
  • Default/Russian Lostbelt Mystic Code (Latter highly reccomended)

Sequence:

  • Cast Illusionary Buff on Arash
  • Arash S3
  • Arash NP

  • Skadi S1 into Qdps
  • Skadi S3 into Qdps
  • Qdps self buffs (if any)
  • Qdps NP

  • Skadi S2
  • Qdps/Sdps atk/crit buffs (if any)
  • One shot all enemies with 100% crit rate on all command cards

I use Lancelot Beserker as my Qdps and Vlad III as my Sdps, which is the ideal setup because they do x1.5 dmg against all classes (Foreigner enemies are still very rare so they don't count)
